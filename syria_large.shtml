03/2011
Protests begin

Perhaps unsurprisingly, data from the UN's refugee agency shows the areas that have seen the largest exodus are those regions that have seen the greatest conflict. Deraa - the starting point of the uprising - and Homs have seen large numbers of people leave. In contrast, the data also appears to show few have left those regions populated by groups known to be government supporters.

More than two million Syrians have fled into neighbouring countries since their country descended into civil war. Official figures show the flow of people began in earnest in early 2012, as widespread protests gave way to armed conflict, and reached a peak in April this year amid claims the government had used chemical weapons.
