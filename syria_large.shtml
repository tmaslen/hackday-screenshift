01/2012
Armed conflict starts

More than two million Syrians have fled into neighbouring countries since their country descended into civil war. Official figures show the flow of people began in earnest in early 2012, as widespread protests gave way to armed conflict, and reached a peak in April this year amid claims the government had used chemical weapons.

Perhaps unsurprisingly, data from the UN's refugee agency shows the areas that have seen the largest exodus are those regions that have seen the greatest conflict. Deraa - the starting point of the uprising - and Homs have seen large numbers of people leave. In contrast, the data also appears to show few have left those regions populated by groups known to be government supporters.

Most of the groups, which range from moderate Islamist to ultraconservative Salafist in outlook, recognise the FSA's Supreme Military Council. However, they are sceptical of the Western-backed opposition National Coalition. The SILF is active in Idlib, Aleppo, Damascus, Homs and Deir al-Zour provinces.

The opposition National Coalition describes Gen Idris as the commander of the FSA, however observers have said the FSA is simply a loose network of brigades rather than a unified fighting force. Brigades supposedly report through the chain of command to Gen Idris, but he is yet to assert operational control and serves more as a spokesman and conduit for foreign funding and arms shipments.

Originally called the Martyrs of Jabal al-Zawiya Brigade, the group was formed in late 2011 in Idlib province. Although its name was changed in mid-2012 to the Martyrs of Syria Brigades to reflect the growing ambitions of its leader
